Vienna May Sauvage

Student at North Island College

I have always loved art even as a young child. I remember collecting supplies and experimenting at home, taking as many art classes as I could, and even spending time in the art rooms when I wasn't in a formal class. I bounced between art styles, projects, and types, for years, never really finding the one thing that I really liked and passing it off as a hobby I only dabbled in while I worked low level jobs.

I went to BCIT and got a Marketing Degree. I realized that it wasn't for me when my favourite class was Ad Design and I loathed all the others. That led me to NIC and their Design and Development prorgram. I have loved every moment of it. Being able to balance logic and creativity has been so fulfilling.

Can You Tell the Difference?

I took DGL 102 last semester and my Term Project was to create a poster for a social justice cause in the style of a famous digital artist. The cause I chose was invasive species and their impact on native ecosystems. This was partially becuase of my volunteering with the amazing ISCBC or Invasive Species Council of BC. You can learn more about all the amazing things they are doing here. The digital artist that I chose was Christopher Dina who is an American digital artist that lives and works in Japan. You can find out more about him by visiting his website here.

Energy

I took FIN 110 last semester and loved it. One of my favourite projects was continuous line drawing. I really took to this style as it let me explore art in a way that I hadn't before and I didn't get hung up on mistakes. Energy is a continuous line drawing of two hands, one on top and one on the bottom, curled towards each other with a single thin line connecting them. I truly believe that energy is something you can see and feel, especially when you put intent into it.
